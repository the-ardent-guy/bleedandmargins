The Field.

What we found

Luxury surfaces are a specification product. Unlike consumer goods, they are rarely chosen by the end buyer, they're specified by architects, interior designers, and developers who source materials to match the vision, durability standards, and sustainability credentials of a project. This makes brand trust not just a marketing concern, but a technical one. The brand that a specifier recommends to a JW Marriott or a Prestige development has to carry the same authority as the space it's going into.

01 India's luxury real estate grew 37.8% in 2024: every square foot needs specifying.

Luxury residential units priced above ₹10 million grew 37.8% year-on-year in 2024, representing up to half of all housing transactions in Mumbai, Bengaluru, and Pune. Each apartment in this bracket specifies 80-120 square metres of surface material, four times the volume of mid-tier dwellings.

02 Sintered stone does what natural stone cannot: and most people don't know that yet.

Sintered stone is scratch-resistant, heat-resistant, stain-resistant, non-porous, and sustainable. It replicates the beauty of natural marble, granite, and slate without the maintenance liabilities that come with quarried stone. Yet the material's superiority remains largely unknown outside the design and architecture community. For Sintero, with a legacy since 1936 and Sacmi Continuum Plus technology, the biggest competitor wasn't another brand. It was unfamiliarity.

03 In luxury surfaces, the catalogue is the product. Before anyone touches the slab, they read the story.

The path to specification in the luxury interior market follows a clear sequence: brand awareness, catalogue or lookbook, specification meeting, project order. For brands targeting architects, developers, and five-star hospitality groups, the quality of the printed catalogue is often the first material proof of the product's quality. If the catalogue feels cheap, the surface does too, regardless of what Sacmi technology went into making it.

The Brief

Give the process the presence it deserves.

Sintero came to us as a brand with genuine depth, a legacy since 1936, world-class Sacmi Continuum Plus technology, and an expanding collection of large-format porcelain slabs inspired by the geological process of sintering itself. What they needed was a brand presence, digital platform, and catalogue system that could stand in rooms alongside the likes of JW Marriott and Prestige Group without apology.

The brief was to translate Sintero's values into a cohesive visual language across every touchpoint: identity, website, digital store, and print catalogues. The material had to feel as considered on the screen and the page as it does on a countertop in a five-star suite.

The Insight

The process is the luxury. Not the finish.

Most surface brands lead with the product, beautiful photography of slabs, room settings, colour ranges. Sintero had all of that. But the real story was the process underneath it: sintering, the geological miracle replicated through engineering. Nature takes millions of years. Sintero does it in days, using extreme pressure and temperature to transform raw material into something denser, harder, and more beautiful than what it started as.

We built the brand around that idea. "Inspired by Nature, Driven by Legacy." The visual language, deep charcoal and warm gold, was designed to feel like the material itself: refined under pressure, beautiful by process, built to outlast the trends around it.
