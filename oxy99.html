The Field.

What the market told us

Portable oxygen is not a niche product — it's a category that spans emergency medicine, high-altitude travel, sports recovery, and senior wellness. In India, the market grew sharply post-pandemic as consumers became aware of oxygen as something you could carry. Oxy99 was already there, already trusted, already with 30 years of manufacturing credibility. The challenge was making the brand reflect what the product actually was.

01 Portable oxygen shifted from emergency product to everyday wellness tool post-2021.

The pandemic made oxygen a household conversation for the first time. Athletes began using canned oxygen for recovery. Trekkers carried it for altitude. Seniors kept it at home. A product that lived in hospital supply chains suddenly had five distinct consumer segments — each requiring different messaging, different placement, and different reasons to buy.

02 Medical credibility is an asset most consumer brands can't buy. Oxy99 had it already.

30 years of manufacturing. Italian process technology. Medically certified. These aren't marketing claims — they're verifiable facts most consumer oxygen brands simply don't have. The packaging and brand communication weren't leveraging any of it. The brand was underselling itself significantly.

03 Last-mile accessibility determines who wins in emergency wellness categories.

Intent to buy oxygen in a moment of need is extremely high — but purchase is abandoned if the product isn't immediately available. Integration into Swiggy Instamart placed Oxy99 in the path of high-intent consumers at the exact moment they searched, with delivery in minutes. Distribution strategy, not just brand strategy, was the growth lever.

The Brief

India's most trusted oxygen brand needed to look like it.

Oxy99 came with 30+ years of manufacturing expertise, Italian process technology from ING. L & A BOSCHI, and genuine medical certification. What it lacked was a brand and packaging presence that communicated any of that at shelf or on screen.

We were brought in across branding, packaging, and performance marketing — with a brief to sharpen the identity, redesign the packaging to carry the brand's credibility clearly, and run campaigns that would put Oxy99 directly in front of the right people at the right moment.

The Insight

Trust already existed. We just had to make it visible.

Most brands spend years building medical credibility. Oxy99 already had it — three decades of it. The gap was purely presentational. The packaging wasn't carrying the brand's authority. The campaigns weren't targeting the specific moments — altitude, emergency, recovery — where the product had a clear and urgent role.

The strategy was straightforward: make the credibility legible on the can, make the brand findable at the moment of need, and make the distribution as immediate as the need itself. Branding, search, and logistics — working together.
