The Field.

What the research showed

India's streetwear market was a niche subculture in 2018. By 2024 it had become one of the fastest-growing segments in Indian fashion, driven by a young, globally-aware consumer base that had outgrown fast fashion and was actively looking for identity in what they wore. The category was expanding, and getting crowded fast.

01 India's streetwear market growing at 10%+ CAGR through 2025. The loudest brands aren't winning long-term.

The market was dominated by graphics-heavy, print-forward brands chasing quarterly trend cycles. Consumer research showed a clear emerging segment: buyers who wanted quality over noise, a brand that didn't need to explain itself season to season. Timelessness was becoming a competitive advantage in a market saturated with the temporary.

02 The enduring brands in fashion don't follow trends. They become the reference point.

Analysis of category-defining apparel brands globally revealed a consistent pattern: the ones that last own a single, clear aesthetic position and hold it with discipline. They don't add when fashion says more is more. Zeroed Out was designed from day one to be that kind of brand.

03 Supima cotton is to apparel what Swiss movement is to watches. It signals without shouting.

Material research pointed to Supima cotton as the foundational asset, grown exclusively in the US, with fibres twice the length of standard cotton, producing a feel that is immediately distinct to anyone who has held it. In a market where the product is all black and the silhouette is intentionally restrained, the quality of the fabric becomes the entire story. The brand identity had to carry that weight.

The Brief

Build an identity for a brand defined entirely by what it removes.

Zeroed Out came to us as a concept - premium all-black apparel, Supima cotton, no colour, no compromise. The collection was already decided. What didn't exist yet was the language to talk about it: a name, a logo, a visual system that could carry a brand built entirely on restraint.

The brief was to map India's streetwear landscape, understand what made brands last, and use those insights to position Zeroed Out as something the market didn't already have: an all-black label with genuine staying power. Bring out the essential. Remove the rest.

The Insight

The name had to be the concept. Not describe it - be it.

Most brands name themselves after aspiration - what they want to feel like. We went a different direction. Zeroed Out ties directly to #000000 - the hex code for true black, the absence of all light, the most precise definition of the colour that defines everything about this brand. The name isn't a metaphor. It's a fact.

It also carries a second meaning: zeroed out as in stripped back, reduced to essence, free of everything non-essential. Precision, focus, the striving for perfection. For a brand built on a single premium material in a single colour, the name had to be as uncompromising as the product. And then the logo had to be built to last - which is why we turned to the golden ratio.

#000000.
Not a colour.
A position.

We identified the growing segment of consumers who had moved past graphic-heavy trend brands and were looking for something with staying power. Zeroed Out was positioned to own that space from launch.

02

Brand Name & Identity Mark

The name Zeroed Out ties directly to #000000 - the hex code for true black - and signals precision, reduction, and permanence. The wordmark pairs a breezy-yet-sharp typeface with a symbol abstracted from the cotton plant, carrying #0 in its form and built on the golden ratio. Name and logo were designed with the same logic: no decoration, no excess - only what is essential and what lasts.
