The Field.

What the research showed

India's streetwear market was a niche subculture in 2018. By 2024 it had become one of the fastest-growing segments in Indian fashion — driven by a young, globally-aware consumer base that had outgrown fast fashion and was actively looking for identity in what they wore. The category was expanding. It was also getting crowded, loud, and increasingly hard to distinguish. That's where the brief began.

01 India's streetwear market growing at 10%+ CAGR through 2025 — but the loudest brands aren't winning long-term.

The market was dominated by graphics-heavy, print-forward brands chasing quarterly trend cycles. Consumer research showed a clear emerging segment — buyers who wanted quality over noise. Premium material, considered design, and a brand that didn't need to explain itself season to season. Timelessness was becoming a competitive advantage in a market saturated with the temporary.

02 The enduring brands in fashion don't follow trends. They become the reference point.

Analysis of category-defining apparel brands — globally and within India — revealed a consistent pattern: the brands that last own a single, clear aesthetic position and hold it with discipline. They don't expand into colour when the market tells them to. They don't add when fashion says more is more. Zeroed Out was designed from day one to be that kind of brand — the one that already knows the answer before the question is asked.

03 Supima cotton is to apparel what Swiss movement is to watches. It signals without shouting.

Material research pointed to Supima cotton as the foundational asset — grown exclusively in the US, with fibres twice the length of standard cotton, producing a feel that is immediately distinct to anyone who has held it. In a market where the product is all black and the silhouette is intentionally restrained, the quality of the fabric becomes the entire story. The brand identity had to carry that weight.

The Brief

Build an identity for a brand defined entirely by what it removes.

Zeroed Out came to us as a concept — premium all-black apparel, Supima cotton, no colour, no compromise. The collection was already decided. What didn't exist yet was the language to talk about it: a name, a logo, a visual system that could carry a brand built entirely on restraint.

The brief was to undertake a comprehensive analysis of India's streetwear landscape — emerging trends, consumer behaviour, the attributes of brands that last — and use those insights to strategically position Zeroed Out as something the market didn't already have: an all-black label that embodied timelessness, bold identity, and lasting impact. Bring out the essential. Remove the rest.

The Insight

The name had to be the concept. Not describe it — be it.

Most brands name themselves after aspiration — what they want to feel like. We went a different direction. Zeroed Out ties directly to #000000 — the hex code for true black, the absence of all light, the most precise definition of the colour that defines everything about this brand. The name isn't a metaphor. It's a fact.

It also carries a second meaning: zeroed out as in stripped back, reduced to essence, free of everything non-essential. Precision, focus, the striving for perfection. For a brand built on a single premium material in a single colour, the name had to be as uncompromising as the product. And then the logo had to be built to last — which is why we turned to the golden ratio.

#000000.
Not a colour.
A position.

The Work.

04 Deliverables
01

Market Research & Strategic Positioning

A full analysis of India's streetwear landscape — consumer behaviour, competitor mapping, category trends, and the white space that existed for a premium all-black label. We identified the growing segment of consumers who had moved past graphic-heavy trend brands and were looking for something with staying power. Zeroed Out was positioned to own that space from launch.

Market Research Consumer Behaviour Competitive Analysis Brand Positioning
02

Naming — Zeroed Out

We explored several naming territories before arriving at Zeroed Out — the one that did more than name the brand, it defined it. The name ties directly to #000000 (the hex code for true black), signals precision and focus, and carries the concept of reduction to essence. Clean, bold, timeless — and mathematically tied to the product it represents.

Brand Naming Concept Development Linguistic Strategy
03

Logo Design — The Cotton Symbol

The logo combines two things: a typographic wordmark in a breezy yet sharp typeface, and a symbol that abstracts the cotton plant — a direct visual reference to the Supima cotton at the heart of the product. The symbol carries #0 in its form, tying back to the hex code. A subtle forest tinge grounds it in natural authenticity. Designed to sit on a collar tag, a swing tag, and a chest — and hold at every scale.

Logo Design Symbol Design Typography Brand Mark
04

Golden Ratio — Built to Last

The logo was constructed on the golden ratio — the mathematical principle found in nature, architecture, and every design that has stood the test of time. For a brand whose entire value proposition is timelessness, building the logo on φ wasn't a design flourish. It was a declaration of intent. The proportions are harmonious by definition. The result feels right without the viewer knowing why — which is exactly how the best logos work.
